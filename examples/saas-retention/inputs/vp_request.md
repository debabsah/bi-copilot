# Request from Priya (VP, Customer Success)

> "Can you build me a churn dashboard for the board? I want monthly **logo churn %**,
> **MRR churn %**, and a **12-month cohort retention curve**, all broken out by plan
> tier. The board meeting is in three weeks. Thanks!"

---

## Answers Priya gave when asked (so the interrogation can run live, not as prep)

- **What decision changes based on this?** "Two things. One, whether we fund a
  dedicated onboarding and CS team next year. Two, what retention story we tell the
  board this quarter."
- **Who acts on it, and how often?** "The exec team and the board. The funding call
  is an annual budget decision. The board sees retention every quarter."
- **What would you do differently if the number were good vs bad?** "If we are
  losing customers early and revenue is not growing inside the base, I fund
  onboarding to fix it. If the base is expanding faster than it churns, I tell the
  board we are healthy and put the money into new-logo growth instead."
- **Why the 12-month cohort retention curve specifically?** "Honestly? The board
  likes a nice chart. I do not think a specific decision rides on the 12-month
  curve. What I actually care about is whether new customers stick in the first few
  months, because that is the part onboarding can move."
- **Do you care about seats and plan changes, or only whether a logo leaves?** "Now
  that you say it, plan changes matter a lot. A customer can stay and cut their
  seats in half. That should count. Logo churn alone would miss it."
