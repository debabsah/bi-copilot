# The number to defend, board readout

- **Claim:** "Net Revenue Retention was **108%** this quarter, up from **102%** a
  year ago. Gross revenue churn improved to **4.2%** monthly. Retention is healthy.
  Recommendation: put next year's budget into new-logo growth rather than funding a
  dedicated onboarding team."
- **Audience:** the board. The sharpest seat is a former CFO who reads the numbers
  closely.
- **Decision riding on it:** the onboarding funding call, and the board's read on
  retention health.

---

## The presenter's prepared answers (so the drill can run)

- **If attacked on the denominator** ("108% of what base?"): "Start-of-period MRR of
  accounts active at the start of the window, account grain, per the locked contract.
  Survivors plus expansion minus contraction and churn, same cohort."
- **If attacked on Finance** ("Finance says total revenue grew about 2%, you say
  retention is 108%, which one is wrong?"): "...NRR is a cohort measure and Finance's
  number is total GL revenue, so they are not the same thing..." (the presenter
  cannot actually state the bridge between the two, or why the gap is that wide).
- **If attacked on whether the definition changed** ("Did you move the goalposts
  since last year?"): "No. This is v1.0 of the contract, effective-dated. Last year's
  102% was recomputed on the same basis."
- **If attacked on the recommendation** ("Your NRR is blended. What is retention for
  accounts in their first 90 days?"): "...I do not have the early-life cohort cut on
  this slide..."
