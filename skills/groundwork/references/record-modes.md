# Ask-the-record modes — groundwork (resume side)

One bright line over all four: **answers come from the record with citations; "the record
is silent on that" is a legitimate, stated answer; a plausible-sounding history that isn't
in the record is confabulation and worse than no answer.**

## Morning brief / open loops ("where did we leave off")
Scan and surface, each with age + citation: pending paste-backs (checks the bench wrote
that never came back — the loop the office otherwise leaves dangling), expired verdicts
(`Re-audit when:` conditions met since), unowned or aging open questions, the last
timeline event, and the single most useful next move. Compact — a screenful, not a report.

## Decision archaeology ("why did we decide X?")
1. Search `decisions.md`, then the timeline (incl. OVERRIDE entries and `by:` lines), then
   the artifact that implemented the decision.
2. Answer with the decision text, its date, owner, and rationale — QUOTED, not paraphrased
   from imagination; link the trail.
3. Found nothing? Say exactly that: "the record is silent — the decision predates the KB
   or was never recorded," plus where else to look (the person named on adjacent events).
   Offer to record the answer once a human supplies it (attributed, dated).

## Handoff package ("package this for my successor")
A curated TOUR of the existing KB, not a rewrite: start-here reading order; the live
contracts with versions and ages; carried verdicts and their `Re-audit when:` status;
open loops with owners; the house rules; the catches ledger (the why behind the
disciplines); known dead ends. Anything NOT in the record is listed as "tribal knowledge
to capture before departure" — named, not invented. Lands as `knowledge-base/handoff.md`.

## Meeting capture (paste raw notes)
Raw notes → CANDIDATE entries only: decisions (owner, rationale), open questions (owner,
ask), timeline events — every attribution marked **confirmed** or **`[unconfirmed]`** (a
name in sloppy notes is not an attribution), every number kept exactly as the notes had
it. The user confirms/edits BEFORE anything is written — minutes never say more than the
meeting did. Unattributable fragments land as `[unconfirmed]` open questions, not facts.
