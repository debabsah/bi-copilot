# Meeting armament — the 30-minute card (mode of defend-my-number)

No time for the full drill; the card is composed FROM THE RECORD (defense sheet if one
exists, otherwise the artifact verdicts, open questions, and timeline), never from
optimism. The card never upgrades a status to make the meeting easier — what NOT to say
is the section that earns its keep.

```markdown
# Armament — <number/finding> · <meeting> · <date>
_From the record as of <date>. Verdict carried: <verdict, age, qualifiers — exactly>._

## The state (one breath)
<value + window + definition pointer + verdict with age>.

## Holding lines (what the evidence supports)
- <line phrased to survive one follow-up, with its citation>.

## Likely attacks → responses
| Attack | Response (one line) | If pressed |
|---|---|---|
| <from the defense sheet / open questions> | <evidence-backed> | <the honest fallback — incl. "we don't know yet, here's when we will"> |

## DO NOT SAY (not settled — do not present as settled)
- <unverified claim / expired verdict / open question> — say instead: "<the honest version>".

## If it goes badly
<the one commitment that is safe to make: the check + the date it lands>.
```

Composition: append to `knowledge-base/defense-sheet.md` (dated); a full drill afterward
upgrades the card. If the number's verdict is expired, the card SAYS so — armament never
papers over a re-audit that is due.
